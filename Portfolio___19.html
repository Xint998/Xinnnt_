Memory

Exhibition, Book, Website

 

My grandfather once told me before he passed away that as long as people remember someone, they never truly disappear from this world. His love and affection during my childhood mean that every time I think of him after his death, waves of sadness and longing immediately overwhelm me. The tears that well up in my eyes and the ache in my nose remind me that these emotions are a result of my brain’s memories. Even though these memories are tinged with sadness, my brain still carefully preserves them for my grandfather. This sparked my curiosity about how the human brain helps us store memories. How long will I cherish these precious memories, and which ones will eventually be forgotten? For my graduation project, I aim to explore the connection between humans and their memories. I have collected the most important memories from people around me and documented them in both a book (tangible memories) and a website (virtual memory archive). This project holds great significance to me.
