UPCYCLING

Size: 4.25 * 5.5 inch  (For small book)

         17 * 5.5 inch  (For brochure)

 

Ying Ying, Xinyu Na, and I delved into the exploration of upcycling and reinvention, culminating in an exhibition set up at Studio 23. For this project, we sourced all our materials, except for printing paper, from recycled sources such as fabrics, plastic bottles, and furniture.

Our project served as a call to action, urging everyone to pay attention to environmental protection issues and harness the power of materials in crafting sustainable designs in their own lives.

Ying Ying utilized materials like plastic bottles and denim fabric to craft a captivating decorative artwork. Her creativity knew no bounds as she breathed new life into these discarded items.

I took apart a broken chair from my home and embarked on a journey of disassembly, cutting, assembly, and finally, painting and decorating. The end result was a small stool created for my mom's little garden. With meticulous attention to detail, I transformed what was once discarded into a functional and aesthetically pleasing piece.

We documented the entire process in a small book, gathering inspiration from online experts who specialize in upcycling and reinvention. Through this endeavor, we aimed to raise awareness about environmental conservation and promote the idea of utilizing materials in creative ways for everyday designs.
